Stages of a Virus

  1. Dormant Phase: Virus program has accessed target user's computer or software but doesn't take action.
    (activated by trigger)
  2. Replication Phase: Virus begins to multiply and replicate itself. Copies are placed into other programs.
  3. Triggering Phase: Virus is activated and performs its intended function.
  4. Execution Phase: Actual program of virus or "payload" is released. Execution can include: deleting files
    on disk, crashing the system, corrupting files, or popping up messages on screen.
