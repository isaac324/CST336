What is a Computer Virus?

A computer virus is a code or program that is
capable of affecting your computer data by
corrupting or destroying them. Virus refers to
any type of malicious software program or "malware".
Viruses can make duplicate copies of itself at a
rapid pace. They spread across every folder and
damage the data of the computer system.



Where did it come from?

Computer can find their way into your computer through many methods:

  • Downloading files from Internet
  • Using a pen drive
  • Opening an email attachment


Components of a Virus

  • Infection Vector: It is something to help the virus spread or replicate. Most have a search routine which
    locates new files or disks to infect.
  • Trigger: A compiled version of the virus that can be activated at any time a file with the virus is run.
  • Payload: It is the actual program/data that performs the virus' purpose. Virus activity is noticeable if
    computer slows down or freezes. Sometimes freezing is the virus' payload.
